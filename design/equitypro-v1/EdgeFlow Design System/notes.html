Cement thesis · Q1FY26

Created 22 Apr 2026 · 09:14 Updated 2 hrs ago Author Anjali Tags #cement #thesis Linked 3 stocks · 2 reports

One-liner: Capacity-utilisation wave + south-region pricing recovery + sector consolidation should re-rate large-cap cement names through Q1–Q3 FY26.

Setup

Industry capacity-utilisation has crossed 72% nationally — the historical level above which cement majors have shown durable pricing power. South India, the laggard for the past 3 quarters, finally moved from ₹325/bag → ₹360/bag in April. The two largest acquirers (UltraTech, Adani Cement) are absorbing distressed assets, reducing fragmentation.

What I'm long

ULTRACEMCO
11,420 · +1.84%
SHREECEM
28,420 · +0.84%
DALBHARAT

Catalysts (next 90 days)

  • Q1FY26 results from UltraTech (29 May) — guidance on FY26 volume +13%
  • South India price action through monsoon — closely watch ICICI Cements channel checks
  • Adani Cement consolidation timeline — likely H2FY26
  • Government infra capex run-rate — already +18% YoY in April
"The cement cycle inflects when utilisation crosses 72%. We're there. The lag from utilisation to pricing is 1–2 quarters." — broker note, 18 Apr

Risk

  • Monsoon failure → demand cliff in H1
  • Coal/petcoke spike → margin headwind
  • South-pricing reversal if new capacity comes online faster

Position

3% portfolio weight on UltraTech as core, 1% each on Shree and Dalmia. Trim 50% if South pricing reverses, exit fully below 200-DMA on UltraTech.
